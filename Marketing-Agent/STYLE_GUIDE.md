## Brand Voice
- **Tone:** Professional, motivating, and community-focused. Not corporate. Not casual.
- **Audience:** Florida-based professionals interested in personal or professional training.
- **Avoid:** Hype language, excessive exclamation points, jargon.
- **Use:** Action verbs, specific details (city names, dates, seat counts), social proof when available.

## Email Subject Lines
- Format: [Benefit or Hook] — [Event/Context]
  - Example: `Get Your Seat — Naples Training, April 12`
  - Example: `One Day. Real Results. Vero Beach is Next.`
- Max 60 characters (mobile preview safe).
- Avoid: "FREE", "!!!", all-caps words (triggers spam filters).
- Always pair with a preview text (85-100 characters) that adds context, not repetition.

## Email Structure
1. **Opening:** Personalized or location-specific hook (1-2 sentences).
2. **Body:** Core message — what the event is, why it matters, what they'll get (2-3 short paragraphs).
3. **CTA:** Single, clear button. Action-oriented text: "Save My Seat", "Register Now", "Learn More".
4. **Closing:** Brief, warm sign-off. Consistent sender name.

## Social Media Posts
- **Facebook:** 2-3 sentences + image. Include event date, city, and link.
- **Instagram:** Shorter caption. Lead with the visual hook. Hashtags at the end (5-8 max).
- **Hashtag pool:** `#VWPM #FloridaTraining #[CityName] #Leadership #PersonalDevelopment`
